## Government Grants

### United States Federal Grants

#### 1. Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program

**Agency:** Multiple (NSF, DoD, DHS, etc.)  
**Award Amount:** Phase I: $50,000-$250,000 | Phase II: $500,000-$1,500,000  
**Duration:** Phase I: 6-12 months | Phase II: 24 months  
**Competition:** Highly competitive  
**Website:** www.sbir.gov

**Eligibility:**
- Small business (<500 employees)
- For-profit company
- U.S. owned and operated (51%+)
- Principal researcher employed by company

**Project Fit:**
- Cybersecurity innovation
- Mobile security technology
- Defensive cyber capabilities
- Penetration testing advancement

**Key Topics:**
- **DHS:** Cybersecurity for critical infrastructure
- **DoD:** Cyber security and resilience
- **NSF:** Cybersecurity research and development
- **DOE:** Cybersecurity for energy sector

**Application Strategy:**
```yaml
Phase I Proposal Focus:
  Title: "Mobile-First Penetration Testing Platform for Critical Infrastructure"
  
  Technical Innovation:
    - Novel mobile security testing approach
    - Automated vulnerability assessment
    - Real-time threat detection
    - Scalable architecture
  
  Commercial Potential:
    - $3.5B market opportunity
    - Growing mobile security needs
    - SMB market accessibility
    - Government applications
  
  Impact:
    - Enhanced security posture
    - Reduced cyber risk
    - Cost-effective solution
    - Scalable deployment

Budget Breakdown:
  Personnel: $100,000 (40%)
  Materials & Equipment: $50,000 (20%)
  Travel: $25,000 (10%)
  Other Direct Costs: $50,000 (20%)
  Indirect Costs: $25,000 (10%)

```

**Timeline:**

- Proposal preparation: 8-12 weeks
- Submission deadline: Varies by agency
- Award notification: 6-9 months
- Project start: 30-60 days after award

## Grant Writing Best Practices

### 1. Research and Preparation

**Before Writing:**
- Thoroughly read RFP/guidelines
- Contact program officer if allowed
- Review successful past applications
- Understand evaluation criteria
- Check eligibility requirements

---

### 2. Compelling Narrative

**Key Elements:**
- Clear problem statement
- Innovative solution
- Measurable impact
- Realistic timeline
- Strong team

**Writing Tips:**
- Use active voice
- Specific, not vague
- Data-driven
- Compelling but honest
- Well-organized

---

### 3. Budget Preparation

**Budget Best Practices:**
- Detailed and justified
- Aligned with activities
- Realistic and defendable
- Match funder priorities
- Include sustainability

---

### 4. Review and Quality Control

**Before Submission:**
- Multiple reviews
- Check all requirements
- Proofread carefully
- Verify all attachments
- Submit early (not last minute)

---

### 5. Follow-up and Stewardship

**After Submission:**
- Thank reviewers
- Respond to questions promptly
- If funded: excellent reporting
- If not funded: request feedback
- Maintain relationships
